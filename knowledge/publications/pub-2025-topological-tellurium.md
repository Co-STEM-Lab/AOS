abstract: "Achieving strong nonlinear optical responses in atomically thin layered materials is essential for advanced nanoscale photonic and on-chip integrated optoelectronic technologies. However, most materials with large second-order susceptibility operate only over narrow wavelength ranges, with rare reports in the mid-infrared region. Topological engineering provides a promising route to enhance nonlinear optical effects, but its potential for broadband harmonic generation remains largely unexplored. Here we report the giant broadband second-harmonic generation in Weyl semiconductor tellurium, enabled by its unique topological electronic structure. The results demonstrate a new paradigm for designing high-efficiency broadband nonlinear optical materials through topological engineering."
